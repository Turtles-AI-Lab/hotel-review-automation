üß† What is an LLM?

A Large Language Model is like having an employee who has read millions of customer service interactions and learned patterns for responding professionally.

Key difference: It doesn't "understand" like humans do‚Äîit recognizes patterns and predicts what words should come next based on training.

üìö Training vs. Inference

Training: Meta trained Llama on billions of text examples (already done - we just use it)

Inference: We give it a review and your brand guidelines, it generates a response (happens in 2-5 seconds)

üéØ Analogy: Think of it like autocomplete on your phone, but instead of predicting the next word, it predicts entire professional responses based on millions of examples.

Understanding Tokenization

What are Tokens?

Tokens are chunks of text the AI uses to process language. Think of them as "building blocks" of words.

Example: Breaking Down a Review

Input Review:

"Great location, but the room was dirty."

Tokenized:

Great location , but the room was dirty .

9 tokens total (roughly 1 token per word, punctuation counts too)

Why It Matters

‚Ä¢ Average review: 50-150 tokens
‚Ä¢ Average response: 100-200 tokens
‚Ä¢ Processing: ~300 tokens per review

Cost Impact

Cloud APIs: Charge per token
Local hosting: Unlimited tokens, $0 cost
